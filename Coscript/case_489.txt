### Step 7
[PLANNING]  
**Action**: Reach Goal (Implementation)  
**Meta-data**: The current goal is to select a non-makeup covering method suitable for light skin. This will be achieved by evaluating options (adhesive skin patches, clothing, temporary tattoo covers) and choosing one based on effectiveness, comfort, and availability for light skin.

[IMP]  
Evaluate options:

2. **Clothing**: Long sleeves, bandages, or wraps can cover tattoos but may be less discreet or practical depending on the tattoo’s location.  
3. **Temporary Tattoo Covers**: Non-makeup temporary covers (e.g., skin-colored stickers) are designed for tattoos but may require precise color matching.  

**Chosen Method**: Adhesive skin patches are selected for their discretion, durability, and compatibility with light skin tones. Select a patch closely matching the skin tone (e.g., fair or light beige).

### Step 8
[PLANNING]  
**Action**: Reach Goal (Implementation)  
**Meta-data**: The current goal is to apply the chosen method (adhesive skin patch) to fully conceal the tattoo. This will be achieved by carefully applying the patch over the tattooed area, ensuring full coverage and a natural appearance.  

[/PLANNING]

[IMP]  
1. Cut the adhesive skin patch (e.g., Tegaderm) to a size slightly larger than the tattoo.  
2. Clean the tattooed area again if needed to ensure no oils interfere with adhesion.  
3. Peel the backing off the patch and place it over the tattoo, smoothing it out to avoid air bubbles or wrinkles.  
4. Press gently to secure the patch, ensuring edges adhere firmly to the skin.  
5. Check that the tattoo is fully covered and the patch blends with the surrounding light skin.  

[/IMP]
