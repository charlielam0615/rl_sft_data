### Step 6: Reach Goal (Implementation)

[PLANNING]  
**Action**: Reach Goal (Implementation)  
**Current Goal**: Prioritize tasks to focus on what matters most.  
**How to Achieve**: Use a prioritization method, such as the Eisenhower Matrix, to categorize tasks by urgency and importance, ensuring critical school tasks are addressed first.  
[/PLANNING]

[IMP]  
To prioritize tasks, apply the Eisenhower Matrix:  
- **Urgent and Important**: Do immediately (e.g., assignments due tomorrow).  
- **Important but Not Urgent**: Schedule (e.g., studying for a test next week).  
- **Urgent but Not Important**: Delegate or minimize (e.g., minor errands).  
- **Not Urgent and Not Important**: Eliminate (e.g., excessive social media).  
Each evening, during the 7:00 PM - 8:00 PM review slot in the daily routine, assess tasks from school (e.g., homework, projects) and extracurriculars. For example, prioritize math homework due tomorrow over a club meeting next week. Update priorities daily to reflect new assignments or deadlines.  
[/IMP]

### Step 11: Reach Goal (Implementation)

[PLANNING]  
**Action**: Reach Goal (Implementation)  
**Current Goal**: Avoid procrastination to stay on schedule.  
**How to Achieve**: Use time-blocking and the Pomodoro Technique to start tasks promptly and maintain focus during study periods.  
[/PLANNING]

[IMP]  
To avoid procrastination:  
- **Time-Blocking**: Assign specific tasks to the 3:00 PM - 5:00 PM study slot from the daily routine, based on the to-do list. For example, allocate 3:00 PM - 3:45 PM for math homework.  
- **Pomodoro Technique**: Work in 25-minute focused intervals with 5-minute breaks. For example, complete two Pomodoros (50 minutes) for math homework, then take a 10-minute break.  
- Start with the highest-priority task (e.g., urgent homework) to build momentum.  
- Minimize distractions by silencing notifications and studying in a quiet space.  
If procrastination occurs, reflect during the 7:00 PM review slot to identify causes (e.g., task overwhelm) and break tasks into smaller steps.  
[/IMP]
