### Step 7
[PLANNING]
**Action: Reach Goal (Implementation)**  
**Meta-data**: The current goal is to practice the roundhouse kick on a heavy bag or kick shield to build technique. This will be achieved by performing controlled kicks with proper form, focusing on stance, pivot, and impact.
[/PLANNING]

[IMP]
To practice the roundhouse kick:  
- Set up a heavy bag or have a partner hold a kick shield.  
- Adopt a fighting stance: feet shoulder-width apart, knees slightly bent, hands up for balance.  
- Execute 3 sets of 10 roundhouse kicks per leg:  
  - Pivot on the supporting foot, rotating the hips.  
  - Extend the kicking leg, striking with the shin or instep.  
  - Return to stance smoothly to maintain balance.  
- Focus on proper technique: ensure the supporting foot pivots fully, hips rotate for power, and the kick lands accurately. Practice for 15-20 minutes, resting 30 seconds between sets. This aligns with martial arts practice for building foundational skills.
[/IMP]

### Step 8
[PLANNING]
**Action: Validation (Implementation)**  
**Meta-data**: The current goal is to validate the roundhouse kick practice to ensure proper technique and safety. This will be achieved by assessing form, balance, and impact accuracy.
[/PLANNING]

[IMP]
To validate the roundhouse kick practice:  
- Check form: Ensure the supporting foot pivots 90-180 degrees, hips rotate fully, and the kicking leg strikes with the shin or instep.  
- Confirm balance: Verify no stumbling or loss of control after each kick.  
- Assess impact: Ensure the kick lands firmly on the heavy bag or shield with a controlled, powerful strike.  
- If possible, record a video or ask a coach/partner for feedback on technique.  
If form or balance is lacking, repeat 1-2 sets focusing on slow, deliberate kicks to correct errors. If validated, proceed to improving speed, power, and technique.
[/IMP]

To improve speed, power, and technique:

- **Technique drill** (5 minutes): Perform 2 sets of 10 slow, controlled kicks per leg, focusing on perfect pivot, hip alignment, and precise impact point.  
These drills, totaling 25 minutes, enhance the roundhouse kick’s effectiveness while adhering to martial arts training principles.
