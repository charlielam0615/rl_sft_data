### Step 3: Implement Nail Hygiene
[PLANNING]  
**Action: Reach Goal (Implementation)**  
**Meta-data**: The current goal is to prevent scratching by maintaining nail hygiene. This will be achieved by trimming nails regularly to reduce the risk of skin damage from scratching.  
[/PLANNING]  

[IMP]  
To prevent scratching, keep nails short and smooth by trimming them weekly with clean nail clippers. File any rough edges to avoid accidental skin irritation. This reduces the ability to scratch effectively, minimizing skin damage and worsening of eczema.  
[/IMP]

### Step 6: Implement Allergen Protection
[PLANNING]  
**Action: Reach Goal (Implementation)**  
**Meta-data**: The current goal is to minimize allergen exposure. This will be achieved by wearing gloves when handling potential allergens like cleaning products, dust, or pet dander.  
[/PLANNING]  

[IMP]  
Wear hypoallergenic, breathable cotton or nitrile gloves during activities that may involve allergen exposure, such as cleaning, gardening, or pet grooming. Ensure gloves are clean and free of irritants before use. This creates a barrier between the skin and potential allergens, reducing eczema flare-ups.  
[/IMP]

---

### Step 7: Maintain Skin Hygiene
[PLANNING]  
**Action: Reach Goal (Implementation)**  
**Meta-data**: The current goal is to maintain skin hygiene to reduce irritation. This will be achieved by keeping the affected area clean and dry using gentle, fragrance-free cleansers.  
[/PLANNING]  

[IMP]  
Wash eczema-affected areas daily with lukewarm water and a gentle, fragrance-free cleanser (e.g., Cetaphil or CeraVe). Pat dry with a soft towel, avoiding rubbing, to prevent irritation. Ensure the skin remains clean and dry to minimize infection risk and irritation.  
[/IMP]

---

### Step 8: Optimize Environmental Moisture
[PLANNING]  
**Action: Reach Goal (Implementation)**  
**Meta-data**: The current goal is to optimize environmental moisture to prevent dryness. This will be achieved by using a humidifier to maintain optimal humidity levels in the living environment.  
[/PLANNING]  

[IMP]  
Place a cool-mist humidifier in your home, especially in the bedroom, and set it to maintain humidity between 40-60%. Clean the humidifier regularly to prevent mold or bacteria growth. This helps keep skin hydrated, reducing dryness-induced itching.  
[/IMP]

---

### Step 9: Soothe Severe Irritation
[PLANNING]  
**Action: Reach Goal (Implementation)**  
**Meta-data**: The current goal is to soothe severe irritation and itchiness. This will be achieved by applying cool, wet compresses to affected areas.  
[/PLANNING]  

[IMP]  
Soak a clean, soft cloth in cool water, wring it out, and apply it to severely irritated areas for 10-15 minutes, 2-3 times daily. Ensure the cloth is clean to avoid infection. This cools the skin, reduces inflammation, and alleviates itching.  
[/IMP]

---

### Step 10: Use Anti-Itch Baths
[PLANNING]  
**Action: Reach Goal (Implementation)**  
**Meta-data**: The current goal is to relieve symptoms with anti-itch baths. This will be achieved by soaking affected areas in a warm anti-itch bath with soothing ingredients.  
[/PLANNING]  

[IMP]  
Prepare a warm (not hot) bath with colloidal oatmeal or baking soda (1 cup per standard tub). Soak affected areas for 15-20 minutes, 1-2 times weekly. Pat dry gently afterward. This calms inflamed skin and reduces itching.  
[/IMP]

---

### Step 11: Moisturize Skin
[PLANNING]  
**Action: Reach Goal (Implementation)**  
**Meta-data**: The current goal is to hydrate skin to reduce eczema symptoms. This will be achieved by applying non-irritating moisturizers to eczema patches.  
[/PLANNING]  

[IMP]  
Apply a fragrance-free, hypoallergenic moisturizer (e.g., Vanicream or Aquaphor) to eczema patches immediately after bathing and 2-3 times daily. Use a thick layer to lock in moisture, ensuring the product is free of common irritants like alcohol or dyes.  
[/IMP]

---

### Step 12: Identify Triggers
[PLANNING]  
**Action: Reach Goal (Implementation)**  
**Meta-data**: The current goal is to identify and avoid personal triggers. This will be achieved by tracking potential triggers and minimizing exposure to them.  
[/PLANNING]  

[IMP]  
Keep a daily journal to note activities, foods, or environmental factors (e.g., pollen, certain fabrics) that precede eczema flare-ups. Common triggers include harsh soaps, dairy, or stress. Once identified, avoid these triggers by choosing hypoallergenic products, adjusting diet, or modifying routines.  
[/IMP]
