### Step 4: Teach Pencil and Paper Handling
[PLANNING]  
**Action: Reach Goal (Implementation)**  
**Meta-data**: The current goal is to teach students how to hold a pencil and a piece of paper. This will be achieved by demonstrating proper grip and paper positioning, using clear instructions and hands-on guidance tailored to kindergarten students.  
[/PLANNING]  

[IMP]  
Show students how to hold a pencil using a tripod grip (thumb, index, and middle fingers). Demonstrate by holding a large pencil and saying, “Hold it like this, not too tight!” Place a piece of paper on the table and show how to keep it steady with the other hand. Walk around to assist students, ensuring they feel confident. Provide oversized pencils if available, as they’re easier for young hands.  
[/IMP]

### Step 6: Guide Hand Tracing
[PLANNING]  
**Action: Reach Goal (Implementation)**  
**Meta-data**: The current goal is to guide students to trace a hand on their paper. This will be achieved by demonstrating the process, providing step-by-step instructions, and assisting students as they trace.  
[/PLANNING]  

[IMP]  
Demonstrate by placing your hand on a piece of paper and tracing around it with a pencil, saying, “Put your hand flat on the paper, like this, and draw around your fingers.” Have each student place their hand on their paper and trace it. Assist students who struggle by holding their paper steady or guiding their pencil. Encourage them to go slowly to stay on track.  
[/IMP]

---

### Step 7: Validate Hand Tracing Progress
[PLANNING]  
**Action: Validation (Implementation)**  
**Meta-data**: The current goal is to check the validity of the hand-tracing activity. This will be achieved by observing students’ tracings to ensure they are following the instructions and producing recognizable hand shapes.  
[/PLANNING]  

[IMP]  
Walk around the classroom to observe each student’s tracing. Check if the lines follow the shape of their hand. Provide positive feedback, like “Great job following your fingers!” For students struggling, gently correct their grip or help reposition their hand. Ensure all students have a recognizable hand shape before moving on.  
[/IMP]

### Step 8: Teach Drawing a Line
[PLANNING]  
**Action: Reach Goal (Implementation)**  
**Meta-data**: The current goal is to have students draw a line from one side of the paper to the other. This will be achieved by demonstrating how to draw a straight line and guiding students to practice.  
[/PLANNING]  

[IMP]  
On a large sheet of paper, demonstrate drawing a straight line from one side to the other, saying, “Start here and draw a line all the way to the other side.” Use a ruler to show a straight edge if needed. Ask students to try on their paper, encouraging them to keep the line as straight as possible. Assist by guiding their hands if they wobble.  
[/IMP]

### Step 11: Teach Drawing a Circle
[PLANNING]  
**Action: Reach Goal (Implementation)**  
**Meta-data**: The current goal is to teach students to draw a circle using a circular template. This will be achieved by providing templates, demonstrating how to trace them, and guiding students to practice.  
[/PLANNING]  

[IMP]  
Distribute circular templates (e.g., lids or stencils) to each student. Demonstrate by placing a template on paper and tracing around it, saying, “Hold the circle and draw around it like this.” Assist students in holding the template steady and tracing carefully. Encourage them to try multiple circles on their paper.  
[/IMP]

### Step 12: Teach Drawing a Square
[PLANNING]  
**Action: Reach Goal (Implementation)**

[IMP]
