**Current Goal**: Understand the rules of pool to identify opportunities for fouls.  
**How to Achieve**: Research and summarize the rules of pool, focusing on common fouls and their consequences, to identify strategic opportunities for committing a foul.  
[/PLANNING]  

[IMP]  
In pool (e.g., 8-ball or 9-ball), key rules include: players must hit their designated balls (solids or stripes in 8-ball) and pocket them legally; the cue ball must strike the player’s ball first; and pocketing the cue ball or failing to hit a rail after contact is a foul. Common fouls include:  
- **Scratch**: Pocketing the cue ball.  
- **Wrong ball**: Hitting the opponent’s ball or the 8-ball prematurely.  
- **No rail contact**: Failing to drive a ball to a rail after contact.  
Consequence: The opponent gets “ball in hand,” allowing them to place the cue ball anywhere. This knowledge helps identify fouls that can be committed strategically.  
[/IMP]
