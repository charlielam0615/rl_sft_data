### Step 6: Reach Goal
[PLANNING]  
**Action: Reach Goal (Implementation)**  
**Current Goal**: Test different incline levels, starting with a lower incline and gradually increasing it, to identify the optimal setting.  
**How to Achieve**: Begin the workout at a low incline (e.g., 1-2%), gradually increase the incline in small increments, and evaluate comfort and workout intensity at each level.  

[/PLANNING]  

[IMP]  
Start the treadmill at a moderate walking or jogging speed (e.g., 3.5-5 mph) with a 1-2% incline for 5 minutes. Increase the incline by 1-2% every 3-5 minutes, up to a maximum of 10-12% or a level that feels challenging but sustainable. Note how each incline level affects heart rate, breathing, and leg fatigue. For example, a 4-6% incline may simulate a moderate hill, while 8-10% provides a more intense challenge. Adjust speed as needed to maintain a safe and effective workout.  

[/IMP]

### Step 7: Validation
[PLANNING]  
**Action: Validation (Implementation)**  
**Current Goal**: Confirm that the tested incline levels provide a challenging yet sustainable workout.  
**How to Achieve**: Assess the physical response to each incline level and ensure the adjustments align with fitness goals (e.g., endurance, fat burning, or strength).  

[/PLANNING]  

[IMP]  
Evaluate each incline level by monitoring heart rate (aim for 60-80% of maximum heart rate for moderate intensity) and perceived exertion (should feel challenging but not exhausting). Ensure the incline adjustments are smooth and the treadmill remains stable. If any incline feels too strenuous or causes discomfort, reduce the incline or speed and try again. Record the incline levels tested and their effects for reference.  

[/IMP]

**Action: Adjust Subgoals (Planning)**

[/PLANNING]

### Step 9: Reach Goal
[PLANNING]  
**Action: Reach Goal (Implementation)**  
**Current Goal**: Finalize the workout plan by selecting the best incline level for an effective treadmill session.  
**How to Achieve**: Choose an incline level that balances challenge and sustainability based on previous testing, and incorporate it into a structured workout routine.  

[/PLANNING]  

[IMP]  
Select an incline level (e.g., 4-6% for moderate intensity or 8-10% for higher intensity) that felt challenging yet manageable during testing. Structure the workout as follows:  
- **Warm-up**: 5-10 minutes at 0% incline, 3-4 mph.  
- **Main Workout**: 20-30 minutes at the chosen incline (e.g., 6%) and a speed that maintains moderate to vigorous intensity (e.g., 4-6 mph).  
- **Cool-down**: 5 minutes at 0% incline, 2-3 mph.  
Adjust the duration or intensity based on fitness level and goals. For variety, alternate incline levels (e.g., 4% for 5 minutes, then 8% for 3 minutes) during the main workout.  

[/IMP]

The plan for achieving the best treadmill workout by adjusting the incline is complete:
