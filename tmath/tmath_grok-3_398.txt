### Problem Restatement
We need to find integer values of \( x \) and \( y \) that satisfy the equation \( xy + 5x + 4y = -5 \), and among all such solutions, determine the greatest possible value of \( y \).

\[ xy + 5x + 4y = -5 \]

\[ -5 - 5x = -5(1 + x) = 5(-x - 1) \]

Thus:  
\[ y = \frac{5(-x - 1)}{x + 4} \]

Alternatively, express the equation in a form to solve for \( x \):  
\[ xy + 5x + 4y = -5 \]

\[ x(y + 5) = -5 - 4y \]

For \( x \) to be an integer, \( y + 5 \) must divide \( -5 - 4y \). This form is also useful, but let’s proceed with the expression for \( y \) first, as it directly relates to maximizing \( y \).
