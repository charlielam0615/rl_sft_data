### Step 2: Reach Goal
[PLANNING]  
**Action: Reach Goal (Implementation)**  
**Current Goal**: Identify the type of geometric shape defined by the equation \( x^2 + y^2 + 6x + 8y = 0 \).  
**How to Achieve**: Analyze the equation by completing the square to determine if it represents a circle, ellipse, or other conic section, and confirm the shape.  
[/PLANNING]

[IMP]  
The equation \( x^2 + y^2 + 6x + 8y = 0 \) resembles the general form of a conic section, likely a circle, since the coefficients of \( x^2 \) and \( y^2 \) are equal. To confirm, rewrite it by completing the square:  

For \( x \)-terms: \( x^2 + 6x = (x + 3)^2 - 9 \).  
For \( y \)-terms: \( y^2 + 8y = (y + 4)^2 - 16 \).  

Substitute into the equation:  
\[ (x + 3)^2 - 9 + (y + 4)^2 - 16 = 0 \]  
\[ (x + 3)^2 + (y + 4)^2 - 25 = 0 \]  
\[ (x + 3)^2 + (y + 4)^2 = 25 \]  

This is the standard form of a circle with center \((-3, -4)\) and radius \( r = \sqrt{25} = 5 \). Thus, the equation defines a circle.  
[/IMP]

### Step 4: Reach Goal
[PLANNING]  
**Action: Reach Goal (Implementation)**  
**Current Goal**: Calculate the area of the circle with radius 5.  
**How to Achieve**: Use the formula for the area of a circle, \( A = \pi r^2 \), where \( r \) is the radius.  
[/PLANNING]

[IMP]  
The radius of the circle is \( r = 5 \). The area of a circle is given by:  
\[ A = \pi r^2 = \pi \cdot 5^2 = 25\pi \]  
Thus, the area enclosed by the circle is \( 25\pi \) square units.  
[/IMP]
