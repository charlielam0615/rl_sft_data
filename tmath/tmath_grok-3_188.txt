### Step 4: Calculate Shaded Area at Each Level

[IMP]
At level \(k\) (where \(k=0\) for the initial square), the square being subdivided has area \(1/9^k\). It is divided into nine squares, each with area \(1/9^{k+1}\). The four corner squares are shaded, so the shaded area at level \(k\) is:
\[
4 \cdot \frac{1}{9^{k+1}} = \frac{4}{9^{k+1}}.
\]
For the Asymptote code, \(n = 3^m\) (where \(m=4, 3, 2, 1, 0\)), the side length is \(2 \cdot 3^m\), but we normalize to the mathematical pattern where the initial square has side length 1. The levels correspond to \(k=0, 1, 2, \ldots\). The shaded areas are:

- Level 1: \(4/9^2 = 4/81\)

### Step 5: Sum the Shaded Areas
[PLANNING]
Action: Reach Goal (Implementation)
Current Goal: Sum the areas of all shaded squares across infinite levels to find the total shaded fraction.
How to Achieve: Recognize the sum as an infinite geometric series and compute its sum.
[/PLANNING]

[IMP]
The total shaded area is the sum of the shaded areas at each level:
\[
\sum_{k=0}^{\infty} \frac{4}{9^{k+1}} = \frac{4}{9} + \frac{4}{9^2} + \frac{4}{9^3} + \cdots = \frac{4}{9} \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} \left(\frac{1}{9}\right)^k.
\]
The series \(\sum_{k=0}^{\infty} (1/9)^k\) is a geometric series with first term 1 and ratio \(1/9\):
\[
\sum_{k=0}^{\infty} \left(\frac{1}{9}\right)^k = \frac{1}{1 - 1/9} = \frac{1}{8/9} = \frac{9}{8}.
\]
Thus, the total shaded area is:
\[
\frac{4}{9} \cdot \frac{9}{8} = \frac{4}{8} = \frac{1}{2}.
\]
Since the total area of the square is 1, the shaded fraction is \(1/2\).
[/IMP]
