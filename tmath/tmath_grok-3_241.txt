### Problem Restatement
We need to find the largest possible value of \( x \) that satisfies the equation \(\frac{5x}{6} + 1 = \frac{3}{x}\), where \( x \) is expressed in the simplified form \( x = \frac{a + b\sqrt{c}}{d} \), with \( a, b, c, d \) being integers. Additionally, we need to compute \(\frac{acd}{b}\).

### Step 2: Reach Goal
[PLANNING]
Action: Reach Goal (Implementation)
Current Goal: Solve the equation \(\frac{5x}{6} + 1 = \frac{3}{x}\) to find all possible values of \( x \).
How to Achieve: Multiply both sides by \( 6x \) to eliminate denominators, resulting in a quadratic equation. Solve the quadratic equation using the quadratic formula.
[/PLANNING]

[IMP]
Start with the equation:
\[
\frac{5x}{6} + 1 = \frac{3}{x}
\]
Multiply both sides by \( 6x \) to clear the denominators:
\[
6x \cdot \frac{5x}{6} + 6x \cdot 1 = 6x \cdot \frac{3}{x}
\]
\[
5x^2 + 6x = 18
\]
Rearrange into standard quadratic form:
\[
5x^2 + 6x - 18 = 0
\]
Solve using the quadratic formula \( x = \frac{-B \pm \sqrt{B^2 - 4AC}}{2A} \), where \( A = 5 \), \( B = 6 \), \( C = -18 \):
\[
\Delta = B^2 - 4AC = 6^2 - 4 \cdot 5 \cdot (-18) = 36 + 360 = 396
\]
\[
x = \frac{-6 \pm \sqrt{396}}{10}
\]
Simplify \(\sqrt{396}\):
\[
396 = 4 \cdot 99 = 4 \cdot 9 \cdot 11 = 36 \cdot 11 \implies \sqrt{396} = \sqrt{36 \cdot 11} = 6\sqrt{11}
\]
Thus:
\[
x = \frac{-6 \pm 6\sqrt{11}}{10} = \frac{6(-1 \pm \sqrt{11})}{10} = \frac{-3 \pm 3\sqrt{11}}{5}
\]
The solutions are:
\[
x_1 = \frac{-3 + 3\sqrt{11}}{5}, \quad x_2 = \frac{-3 - 3\sqrt{11}}{5}
\]
[/IMP]

### Step 3: Validation
[PLANNING]
Action: Validation (Implementation)

How to Achieve: Substitute both \( x_1 \) and \( x_2 \) back into \(\frac{5x}{6} + 1 = \frac{3}{x}\) and check if the equation holds. Confirm the solutions are in the form \(\frac{a + b\sqrt{c}}{d}\).
[/PLANNING]

[IMP]
**Check \( x_1 = \frac{-3 + 3\sqrt{11}}{5} \):**
Left-hand side:
\[
\frac{5}{6} \cdot \frac{-3 + 3\sqrt{11}}{5} + 1 = \frac{-3 + 3\sqrt{11}}{6} + 1 = \frac{-3 + 3\sqrt{11} + 6}{6} = \frac{3 + 3\sqrt{11}}{6} = \frac{1 + \sqrt{11}}{2}
\]
Right-hand side:
\[
\frac{3}{\frac{-3 + 3\sqrt{11}}{5}} = \frac{3 \cdot 5}{-3 + 3\sqrt{11}} = \frac{15}{-3 + 3\sqrt{11}}
\]
Rationalize the denominator:
\[
\frac{15}{-3 + 3\sqrt{11}} \cdot \frac{-3 - 3\sqrt{11}}{-3 - 3\sqrt{11}} = \frac{15(-3 - 3\sqrt{11})}{(-3 + 3\sqrt{11})(-3 - 3\sqrt{11})} = \frac{15(-3 - 3\sqrt{11})}{9 - 9 \cdot 11} = \frac{15(-3 - 3\sqrt{11})}{9 - 99} = \frac{15(-3 - 3\sqrt{11})}{-90} = \frac{-3 - 3\sqrt{11}}{-6} = \frac{3 + 3\sqrt{11}}{6} = \frac{1 + \sqrt{11}}{2}
\]
Both sides match, so \( x_1 \) is valid.

**Check \( x_2 = \frac{-3 - 3\sqrt{11}}{5} \):**
Left-hand side:
\[
\frac{5}{6} \cdot \frac{-3 - 3\sqrt{11}}{5} + 1 = \frac{-3 - 3\sqrt{11}}{6} + 1 = \frac{-3 - 3\sqrt{11} + 6}{6} = \frac{3 - 3\sqrt{11}}{6} = \frac{1 - \sqrt{11}}{2}
\]
Right-hand side:
\[
\frac{3}{\frac{-3 - 3\sqrt{11}}{5}} = \frac{15}{-3 - 3\sqrt{11}}
\]
Rationalize:
\[
\frac{15}{-3 - 3\sqrt{11}} \cdot \frac{-3 + 3\sqrt{11}}{-3 + 3\sqrt{11}} = \frac{15(-3 + 3\sqrt{11})}{9 - 99} = \frac{15(-3 + 3\sqrt{11})}{-90} = \frac{-3 + 3\sqrt{11}}{-6} = \frac{3 - 3\sqrt{11}}{6} = \frac{1 - \sqrt{11}}{2}
\]
Both sides match, so \( x_2 \) is valid.

Both solutions are in the form \(\frac{a + b\sqrt{c}}{d}\):
- For \( x_1 = \frac{-3 + 3\sqrt{11}}{5} \), \( a = -3 \), \( b = 3 \), \( c = 11 \), \( d = 5 \).
- For \( x_2 = \frac{-3 - 3\sqrt{11}}{5} \), \( a = -3 \), \( b = -3 \), \( c = 11 \), \( d = 5 \).
[/IMP]
