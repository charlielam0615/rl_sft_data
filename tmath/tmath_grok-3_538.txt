### Problem Statement
I need to determine the initial investment amount (present value) required to grow to $500,000 in 10 years at an annually compounded interest rate of 5%. The answer should be expressed as a dollar value rounded to the nearest cent.

### Step 2: Reach Goal
[PLANNING]  
**Action: Reach Goal (Implementation)**  
**Meta-data**: The current goal is to identify and apply the compound interest formula to calculate the initial investment (present value). This will be achieved by using the compound interest formula, plugging in the known values (future value, interest rate, time), and solving for the present value.  
[/PLANNING]

[IMP]  
The compound interest formula is:  
\[ FV = PV \cdot (1 + r)^n \]  
where:  
- \( FV \) = future value ($500,000),  
- \( PV \) = present value (initial investment, unknown),  
- \( r \) = annual interest rate (5% = 0.05),  
- \( n \) = number of years (10).  

Rearrange to solve for \( PV \):  
\[ PV = \frac{FV}{(1 + r)^n} \]  

Substitute the known values:  
\[ PV = \frac{500,000}{(1 + 0.05)^{10}} \]  
\[ PV = \frac{500,000}{(1.05)^{10}} \]  

Calculate \( (1.05)^{10} \):  
\[ (1.05)^{10} \approx 1.62889462678 \]  

Now compute:  
\[ PV = \frac{500,000}{1.62889462678} \approx 307,045.7866 \]  

The present value is approximately $307,045.7866.  
[/IMP]
