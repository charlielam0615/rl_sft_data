### Problem Statement
We are given a table of values for a function \( f(x) \):

| \( x \) | 1  | 2  | 3  | 5  | 8  | 13 |
|---------|----|----|----|----|----|----|
| \( f(x) \) | 3  | 13 | 8  | 1  | 0  | 5  |

We need to find \( f^{-1}\left( \frac{f^{-1}(5) + f^{-1}(13)}{f^{-1}(1)} \right) \), provided that the inverse function \( f^{-1} \) exists.

---

[PLANNING]
**Action: Forward Subgoaling (Planning)**  
Break down the problem into intermediate subgoals to compute the final expression.  
1. Verify that \( f^{-1} \) exists by checking if \( f(x) \) is one-to-one (injective).  
2. Compute \( f^{-1}(5) \), \( f^{-1}(13) \), and \( f^{-1}(1) \).  
3. Evaluate the expression \( \frac{f^{-1}(5) + f^{-1}(13)}{f^{-1}(1)} \).  
4. Compute \( f^{-1} \) of the result from step 3.
[/PLANNING]

- **How to Achieve:** Check if \( f(x) \) is one-to-one by ensuring all \( f(x) \) values in the table are distinct.

List of \( f(x) \) values: \( 3, 13, 8, 1, 0, 5 \).  
All values are distinct, so \( f(x) \) is one-to-one, and \( f^{-1} \) exists.
[/IMP]

- For \( f^{-1}(1) \): Find \( x \) such that \( f(x) = 1 \). Since \( f(5) = 1 \), \( f^{-1}(1) = 5 \).
