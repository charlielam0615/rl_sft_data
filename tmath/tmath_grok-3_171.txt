[IMP]
The Asymptote code defines \( f(y) = \frac{(y-2)^2}{2} - 4 \). Simplify this:  
\[
f(y) = \frac{(y^2 - 4y + 4)}{2} - 4 = \frac{y^2}{2} - 2y + 2 - 4 = \frac{y^2}{2} - 2y - 2}
\]
The graph is reflected over the line \( y = x \), which swaps \( x \) and \( y \). Thus, the original graph represents \( x = f(y) \), and after reflection, we get \( y = f(x) \), or equivalently, \( x = f^{-1}(y) \). We need the equation in the form \( x = ay^2 + by + c \), so we interpret the reflected graph as \( x \) as a function of \( y \).

Since the reflection over \( y = x \) swaps coordinates, the original graph plots \( (f(y), y) \), and the reflected graph plots \( (y, f(y)) \), meaning \( y = f(x) \). We need to find the inverse function where \( x = f(y) \), and express \( y \) in terms of \( x \), then convert to \( x = ay^2 + by + c \).
[/IMP]

### Step 3: Reach Goal
[PLANNING]
**Action: Reach Goal (Implementation)**  
**Meta-data**:  
- **Current Goal**: Derive the equation of the reflected parabola in the form \( x = ay^2 + by + c \).  
- **How to Achieve**: Start with \( x = f(y) = \frac{y^2}{2} - 2y - 2 \), find the inverse by solving for \( y \) in terms of \( x \), and rewrite the equation.  
[/PLANNING]

[IMP]
Start with the original function:  
\[
x = \frac{y^2}{2} - 2y - 2
\]
To find the inverse, solve for \( y \):  
\[
x = \frac{y^2}{2} - 2y - 2
\]
Multiply through by 2 to clear the fraction:  
\[
2x = y^2 - 4y - 4
\]
Rearrange into a quadratic equation in \( y \):  
\[
y^2 - 4y - 4 - 2x = 0
\]
\[
y^2 - 4y = 2x + 4
\]
Complete the square for \( y \):  
\[
y^2 - 4y + 4 = 2x + 4 + 4
\]
\[
(y - 2)^2 = 2x + 8
\]
\[
y - 2 = \pm \sqrt{2x + 8}
\]
\[
y = 2 \pm \sqrt{2x + 8}
\]
Since the parabola opens to the right (as it’s reflected from a parabola opening upward), we need \( x \) as a function of \( y \). Notice that the original equation \( x = \frac{y^2}{2} - 2y - 2 \) is already in the form \( x = ay^2 + by + c \). Let’s confirm by comparing:  
\[
x = \frac{1}{2}y^2 - 2y - 2
\]
Here, \( a = \frac{1}{2} \), \( b = -2 \), \( c = -2 \). The reflection over \( y = x \) means the graph represents this equation directly, as the Asymptote code’s reflection transforms \( x = f(y) \) to match the form required.
[/IMP]
