- At least one of the 3 slots must be Algebra or Geometry.

- **Case 1: Exactly 1 mathematics course** (either Algebra or Geometry).
- **Case 2: Exactly 2 mathematics courses** (both Algebra and Geometry).

**Case 1: Exactly 1 mathematics course**
- Choose 1 mathematics course from {Algebra, Geometry}: \( \binom{2}{1} = 2 \) ways.
- The remaining 2 slots are filled from the 4 non-English, non-mathematics courses {History, Art, Latin} or the remaining mathematics course.
- Available courses for the remaining 2 slots: {Algebra or Geometry (the one not chosen), History, Art, Latin} (4 courses).
- Choose 2 courses from these 4: \( \binom{4}{2} = 6 \) ways.
- Total for Case 1: \( 2 \times 6 = 12 \) ways.

**Case 2: Exactly 2 mathematics courses**
- Choose both Algebra and Geometry: \( \binom{2}{2} = 1 \) way.
- The remaining 1 slot is filled from the 3 non-mathematics courses {History, Art, Latin}.
- Choose 1 course from these 3: \( \binom{3}{1} = 3 \) ways.
- Total for Case 2: \( 1 \times 3 = 3 \) ways.

**Total number of programs**:
- Add the two cases: \( 12 + 3 = 15 \).

Thus, there are 15 possible course programs for this case.
