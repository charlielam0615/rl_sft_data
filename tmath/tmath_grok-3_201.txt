### Step 2: Reach Goal

[PLANNING]
**Action: Reach Goal (Implementation)**  
**Meta-data:**  
- **Current Goal:** Determine the condition for the quadratic equation \( x^2 + ax + 8a = 0 \) to have integer solutions for \( x \).  
- **How to Achieve:** Use the quadratic formula to find the roots of the equation and ensure they are integers. This involves checking that the discriminant is a perfect square and the resulting roots are integers.

[IMP]
The quadratic equation is \( x^2 + ax + 8a = 0 \), with coefficients:  
- \( A = 1 \),  
- \( B = a \),  
- \( C = 8a \).  

The roots are given by the quadratic formula:  
\[ x = \frac{-B \pm \sqrt{B^2 - 4AC}}{2A} = \frac{-a \pm \sqrt{a^2 - 4 \cdot 1 \cdot 8a}}{2 \cdot 1} = \frac{-a \pm \sqrt{a^2 - 32a}}{2}. \]  

For \( x \) to be an integer, two conditions must hold:  
1. The discriminant \( \Delta = a^2 - 32a \) must be a perfect square, say \( k^2 \), for some integer \( k \).  
2. The numerator \( -a \pm \sqrt{a^2 - 32a} \) must be divisible by the denominator \( 2 \), so \( -a \pm k \) must be even.  

Thus, we need:  
\[ a^2 - 32a = k^2, \]  
and \( -a \pm k \) even for the roots:  
\[ x = \frac{-a \pm k}{2}. \]  
[/IMP]

### Step 3: Reach Goal

[PLANNING]
**Action: Reach Goal (Implementation)**  
**Meta-data:**  
- **Current Goal:** Find integer values of \( a \) that satisfy \( a^2 - 32a = k^2 \) and ensure the roots are integers.  
- **How to Achieve:** Rewrite the discriminant condition as a quadratic in \( a \), solve for integer pairs \( (a, k) \), and check if the roots \( x = \frac{-a \pm k}{2} \) are integers.  
[/PLANNING]

[IMP]
Rewrite the discriminant condition:  
\[ a^2 - 32a = k^2. \]  
Rearrange:  
\[ a^2 - 32a - k^2 = 0. \]  
Complete the square for \( a \):  
\[ a^2 - 32a = (a - 16)^2 - 256, \]  
so:  
\[ (a - 16)^2 - 256 = k^2, \]  
\[ (a - 16)^2 - k^2 = 256. \]  

This is a difference of squares:  
\[ ((a - 16) - k)((a - 16) + k) = 256. \]  
Let:  
- \( u = a - 16 - k \),  
- \( v = a - 16 + k \).  

Then:  
\[ u \cdot v = 256, \]  
and solving for \( a \) and \( k \):  
- Add: \( u + v = (a - 16 - k) + (a - 16 + k) = 2(a - 16) \), so \( a = 16 + \frac{u + v}{2} \).  
- Subtract: \( v - u = (a - 16 + k) - (a - 16 - k) = 2k \), so \( k = \frac{v - u}{2} \).  

For \( a \) and \( k \) to be integers, \( u + v \) and \( v - u \) must be even (so their halves are integers). Since \( u \cdot v = 256 \), consider factor pairs \( (u, v) \) of 256 (including negatives), ensuring both sums and differences are even. The positive factor pairs of 256 are:  
\[ (1, 256), (2, 128), (4, 64), (8, 32), (16, 16), (32, 8), (64, 4), (128, 2), (256, 1). \]  

Also consider negative pairs: \( (-u, -v) \). Check pairs where \( u \) and \( v \) have the same parity (both even or both odd):  
- **Pair (2, 128):** Both even.  
  - \( a = 16 + \frac{2 + 128}{2} = 16 + 65 = 81 \).  
  - \( k = \frac{128 - 2}{2} = 63 \).  
  - Check: \( a^2 - 32a = 81^2 - 32 \cdot 81 = 6561 - 2592 = 3969 = 63^2 \).  
  - Roots: \( x = \frac{-81 \pm 63}{2} = \frac{-18}{2} = -9 \), \( \frac{-144}{2} = -72 \). Both integers.  

- **Pair (8, 32):** Both even.  
  - \( a = 16 + \frac{8 + 32}{2} = 16 + 20 = 36 \).  
  - \( k = \frac{32 - 8}{2} = 12 \).  
  - Check: \( 36^2 - 32 \cdot 36 = 1296 - 1152 = 144 = 12^2 \).  
  - Roots: \( x = \frac{-36 \pm 12}{2} = \frac{-24}{2} = -12 \), \( \frac{-48}{2} = -24 \). Both integers.  

- **Pair (16, 16):** Both even.  
  - \( a = 16 + \frac{16 + 16}{2} = 16 + 16 = 32 \).  
  - \( k = \frac{16 - 16}{2} = 0 \).  
  - Check: \( 32^2 - 32 \cdot 32 = 1024 - 1024 = 0 = 0^2 \).  
  - Roots: \( x = \frac{-32 \pm 0}{2} = -16 \). Single integer root (double root).  

- **Pair (-2, -128):** Both even.  
  - \( a = 16 + \frac{-2 - 128}{2} = 16 - 65 = -49 \).  
  - \( k = \frac{-128 - (-2)}{2} = \frac{-126}{2} = -63 \).  
  - Check: \( (-49)^2 - 32 \cdot (-49) = 2401 + 1568 = 3969 = 63^2 \).  
  - Roots: \( x = \frac{-(-49) \pm (-63)}{2} = \frac{49 - 63}{2} = -7 \), \( \frac{49 + 63}{2} = 56 \). Both integers.  

- **Pair (-8, -32):** Both even.  
  - \( a = 16 + \frac{-8 - 32}{2} = 16 - 20 = -4 \).  
  - \( k = \frac{-32 - (-8)}{2} = \frac{-24}{2} = -12 \).  
  - Check: \( (-4)^2 - 32 \cdot (-4) = 16 + 128 = 144 = 12^2 \).  
  - Roots: \( x = \frac{-(-4) \pm (-12)}{2} = \frac{4 - 12}{2} = -4 \), \( \frac{4 + 12}{2} = 8 \). Both integers.  

Other pairs (e.g., (1, 256), (4, 64)) yield odd sums or differences, leading to non-integer \( a \) or \( k \).  
[/IMP]
