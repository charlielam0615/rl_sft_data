### Problem Statement
Celeste has an o-Pod MP3 player with 10 songs, each with a different length. The shortest song is 30 seconds, and each subsequent song is 30 seconds longer than the previous one. Her favorite song is 3 minutes 30 seconds (210 seconds) long. The o-Pod plays all 10 songs in random order without repetition. We need to find the probability that the first 4 minutes 30 seconds (270 seconds) of music played does not include every second of her favorite song. The answer should be expressed as a common fraction.

### Step 8
[PLANNING]
**Action: Reach Goal (Implementation)**  
**Current Goal**: Calculate the total number of song orderings and identify favorable sequences where the favorite song is not fully played in 270 seconds.  
**How to Achieve**: Use permutations to count total sequences and analyze the favorite song’s starting time.

[IMP]
**Total Sequences**:
The o-Pod plays all 10 songs in random order, so there are 10! = 3,628,800 possible permutations.

**Favorable Sequences**:
The favorite song (210s) completes only if it starts at or before 270 − 210 = 60 seconds. We want sequences where it either:
- Starts after 60 seconds, or
- Is not played within 270 seconds.

**Complementary Approach** (easier):
Calculate the probability that the favorite song *does* play completely (starts at or before 60s), then subtract from 1.

- **Favorite song in position 1**: Starts at 0s, plays fully (0 + 210 ≤ 270).
- **Position 2**: Preceded by one song. Minimum duration of any song is 30s, so it starts at 30s (30 + 210 = 240 ≤ 270).
- **Position 3**: Preceded by two songs. Minimum duration = 30 + 60 = 90s, so it starts at 90s (90 + 210 = 300 > 270). Cannot complete.

Thus, the favorite song completes only if it is in position 1 or 2.
- Number of sequences with favorite song in position 1: 1 choice for position 1, 9! for the rest = 9! = 362,880.
- Position 2: 1 choice for position 2, 1 for position 1 (any of 9 songs), 8! for the rest = 9 × 8! = 9 × 40,320 = 362,880.
- Total unfavorable sequences: 362,880 + 362,880 = 725,760.

**Favorable sequences**: 10! − 725,760 = 3,628,800 − 725,760 = 2,903,040.

**Probability**:
\[ \text{Probability} = \frac{\text{Favorable sequences}}{\text{Total sequences}} = \frac{2,903,040}{3,628,800} \]

Simplify:
\[ 2,903,040 \div 3,628,800 = \frac{2,903,040 \div 1,814,400}{3,628,800 \div 1,814,400} = \frac{1.6}{2} = \frac{8}{10} = \frac{4}{5} \]
(Checking divisibility: 3,628,800 ÷ 9 = 403,200; 2,903,040 ÷ 9 = 322,560; then 322,560 ÷ 80,640 = 4, 403,200 ÷ 80,640 = 5.)
[/IMP]
