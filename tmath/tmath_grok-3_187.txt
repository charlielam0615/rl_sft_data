### Problem Statement
We are given a rectangular box with a volume of 4320 cubic inches, a surface area of 1704 square inches, and the sum of the lengths of its 12 edges equal to 208 inches. We need to find the volume of the box, in cubic inches, if its length, width, and height are each increased by one inch.

### Step 2: Reach Goal
[PLANNING]
**Action: Reach Goal (Implementation)**  
**Meta-data:**  
- **Current goal:** Determine the original dimensions (\( l \), \( w \), \( h \)) of the box.  
- **How to achieve it:** Use the given equations for volume (\( lwh = 4320 \)), surface area (\( 2(lw + lh + wh) = 1704 \)), and sum of edge lengths (\( 4(l + w + h) = 208 \)) to set up a system of equations and solve for \( l \), \( w \), and \( h \).  
[/PLANNING]

[IMP]
From the sum of the edges:  
\[ 4(l + w + h) = 208 \implies l + w + h = 52 \]  

From the surface area:  
\[ 2(lw + lh + wh) = 1704 \implies lw + lh + wh = 852 \]  

From the volume:  
\[ lwh = 4320 \]  

We have the system:  
1. \( l + w + h = 52 \)  
2. \( lw + lh + wh = 852 \)  
3. \( lwh = 4320 \)  

Assume the dimensions are positive integers (since the numbers are whole and the context suggests a practical box). Let’s use the volume equation to express one variable. From equation 3:  
\[ lwh = 4320 \]  
So, \( h = \frac{4320}{lw} \). Substitute into equation 1:  
\[ l + w + \frac{4320}{lw} = 52 \]  
Multiply through by \( lw \):  
\[ l^2 w + lw^2 + 4320 = 52lw \]  
Rearrange:  
\[ l^2 w + lw^2 - 52lw + 4320 = 0 \]  
\[ lw(l + w - 52) + 4320 = 0 \]  
\[ lw(52 - (l + w)) = 4320 \]  
Since \( l + w + h = 52 \), let \( h = 52 - (l + w) \). Then:  
\[ lw h = 4320 \implies lw (52 - (l + w)) = 4320 \]  
Let \( s = l + w \). Then \( h = 52 - s \), and:  
\[ lw (52 - s) = 4320 \]  
Since \( lwh = 4320 \), and \( h = 52 - s \), we need \( lw = \frac{4320}{52 - s} \). Also, from equation 2:  
\[ lw + lh + wh = 852 \]  
Substitute \( h = 52 - s \):  
\[ lw + l(52 - s) + w(52 - s) = 852 \]  
Since \( s = l + w \):  
\[ lw + (l + w)(52 - s) = 852 \]  
\[ lw + s(52 - s) = 852 \]  
\[ lw = 852 - s(52 - s) = 852 - 52s + s^2 \]  
Now, equate with \( lw = \frac{4320}{52 - s} \):  
\[ s^2 - 52s + 852 = \frac{4320}{52 - s} \]  
Multiply both sides by \( 52 - s \):  
\[ (s^2 - 52s + 852)(52 - s) = 4320 \]  
Let \( t = 52 - s \), so \( s = 52 - t \). Substitute:  
\[ ((52 - t)^2 - 52(52 - t) + 852)t = 4320 \]  
Compute:  
\[ (52 - t)^2 = t^2 - 104t + 2704 \]  
\[ -52(52 - t) = -2704 + 52t \]  
\[ (52 - t)^2 - 52(52 - t) + 852 = t^2 - 104t + 2704 - 2704 + 52t + 852 = t^2 - 52t + 852 \]  
So:  
\[ (t^2 - 52t + 852)t = t^3 - 52t^2 + 852t = 4320 \]  
\[ t^3 - 52t^2 + 852t - 4320 = 0 \]  
Solve this cubic equation. Test possible integer roots (factors of 4320). Try \( t = 6 \):  
\[ 6^3 - 52 \cdot 6^2 + 852 \cdot 6 - 4320 = 216 - 1872 + 5112 - 4320 = 936 - 936 = 0 \]  
So, \( t = 6 \) is a root. Then \( s = 52 - t = 52 - 6 = 46 \). Since \( s = l + w \), we have \( l + w = 46 \), and \( h = t = 6 \). Now, \( lw = \frac{4320}{h} = \frac{4320}{6} = 720 \). Solve:  
\[ l + w = 46, \quad lw = 720 \]  
\[ l(46 - l) = 720 \implies l^2 - 46l + 720 = 0 \]  
Discriminant: \( 46^2 - 4 \cdot 720 = 2116 - 2880 = -764 \). No real solutions. Let’s try another root for the cubic. Try \( t = 10 \):  
\[ 10^3 - 52 \cdot 10^2 + 852 \cdot 10 - 4320 = 1000 - 5200 + 8520 - 4320 = 9520 - 9520 = 0 \]  
So, \( t = 10 \). Then \( s = 52 - 10 = 42 \), \( h = 10 \), \( l + w = 42 \), \( lw = \frac{4320}{10} = 432 \). Solve:  
\[ l^2 - 42l + 432 = 0 \]  
Discriminant: \( 42^2 - 4 \cdot 432 = 1764 - 1728 = 36 \). Roots:  
\[ l = \frac{42 \pm \sqrt{36}}{2} = \frac{42 \pm 6}{2} \]  
\[ l = 24, \quad w = 18 \quad (\text{or vice versa}) \]  
Dimensions: \( l = 24 \), \( w = 18 \), \( h = 10 \). Verify:  
- Volume: \( 24 \cdot 18 \cdot 10 = 4320 \).  
- Surface area: \( 2(24 \cdot 18 + 24 \cdot 10 + 18 \cdot 10) = 2(432 + 240 + 180) = 2 \cdot 852 = 1704 \).  
- Edges: \( 4(24 + 18 + 10) = 4 \cdot 52 = 208 \).  
All conditions satisfied.  
[/IMP]
