**Current Goal**: Calculate the number of paths from \( E \) to \( F \) and from \( F \) to \( G \), and combine them to find the total number of 9-step paths.  
**How to Achieve**: Use the formula for lattice paths. For a path with \( r \) right steps and \( d \) down steps, the number of paths is given by the binomial coefficient \( \binom{r + d}{r} \). Compute this for both segments and multiply the results, as the paths are independent.
[/PLANNING]

[IMP]
- **From \( E (0, 4) \) to \( F (3, 3) \)**: 3 right, 1 down, total 4 steps.
  - Number of paths: \( \binom{3 + 1}{3} = \binom{4}{3} = \frac{4!}{3!1!} = 4 \).
- **From \( F (3, 3) \) to \( G (5, 0) \)**: 2 right, 3 down, total 5 steps.
  - Number of paths: \( \binom{2 + 3}{2} = \binom{5}{2} = \frac{5!}{2!3!} = \frac{120}{2 \cdot 6} = 10 \).
- **Total paths**: Since the paths from \( E \) to \( F \) and \( F \) to \( G \) are independent, multiply the number of paths:
  - \( 4 \times 10 = 40 \).

Thus, there are 40 paths from \( E \) to \( F \) to \( G \).
