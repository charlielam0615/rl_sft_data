### Problem Restatement

\[

\[

\]

- **Distance \( BC \)**: From \( B(3, 8) \) to \( C \left( \frac{-k + 3}{k + 1}, \frac{8}{k + 1} \right) \):

\[
BC = \sqrt{\left( 3 - \frac{-k + 3}{k + 1} \right)^2 + \left( 8 - \frac{8}{k + 1} \right)^2}

Simplify the \( x \)-coordinate difference:

\[
3 - \frac{-k + 3}{k + 1} = \frac{3(k + 1) - (-k + 3)}{k + 1} = \frac{3k + 3 + k - 3}{k + 1} = \frac{4k}{k + 1}

Simplify the \( y \)-coordinate difference:

\[
8 - \frac{8}{k + 1} = \frac{8(k + 1) - 8}{k + 1} = \frac{8k + 8 - 8}{k + 1} = \frac{8k}{k + 1}

\[

Apply the condition \( AC = 3 \cdot BC \):

\[
\frac{4\sqrt{5}}{k + 1} = 3 \cdot \frac{4\sqrt{5} \cdot k}{k + 1}
\]

Since \( 4\sqrt{5} \neq 0 \) and \( k + 1 \neq 0 \), divide both sides by \( \frac{4\sqrt{5}}{k + 1} \):

\[
1 = 3k
\]

\[
k = \frac{1}{3}
\]

Substitute \( k = \frac{1}{3} \) into \( C \)'s coordinates:

\[

\[
C_y = \frac{8}{\frac{1}{3} + 1} = \frac{8}{\frac{4}{3}} = 8 \cdot \frac{3}{4} = 6

Thus, the coordinates of \( C \) are \( (2, 6) \).
