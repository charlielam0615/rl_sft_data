### Step 2: Reach Goal

**Action**: Reach Goal (Implementation)

**Current Goal**: Expand \( (1 - 2x^2)^5 \) using the binomial theorem and identify the term with \( x^4 \).

**How to Achieve**: Use the binomial theorem for \( (a + b)^n \), where \( a = 1 \), \( b = -2x^2 \), and \( n = 5 \). The general term in the expansion is given by \( \binom{n}{k} a^{n-k} b^k \). Substitute the values, compute the general term, and find the value of \( k \) that produces \( x^4 \).
[/PLANNING]

[IMP]
The binomial expansion of \( (1 - 2x^2)^5 \) is:

\[ (1 + (-2x^2))^5 = \sum_{k=0}^5 \binom{5}{k} (1)^{5-k} (-2x^2)^k \]

Simplify the general term:

\[ \binom{5}{k} (1)^{5-k} (-2x^2)^k = \binom{5}{k} (-2)^k (x^2)^k = \binom{5}{k} (-2)^k x^{2k} \]

To find the term with \( x^4 \), set the exponent of \( x \):

\[ 2k = 4 \]

\[ k = 2 \]

For \( k = 2 \), the term is:

\[ \binom{5}{2} (-2)^2 x^{2 \cdot 2} = \binom{5}{2} \cdot 4 \cdot x^4 \]

Calculate:

\[ \binom{5}{2} = \frac{5!}{2! \cdot 3!} = \frac{5 \cdot 4}{2 \cdot 1} = 10 \]

\[ (-2)^2 = 4 \]

\[ \text{Coefficient} = 10 \cdot 4 = 40 \]

Thus, the coefficient of \( x^4 \) is 40.
[/IMP]
