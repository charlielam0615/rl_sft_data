### Step 7
[PLANNING]  
Action: Reach Goal (Implementation)  
Current Goal: Solve the equation for \(x\).  
How to Achieve: Simplify the equation \(\frac{20}{2x + 1} + \frac{1}{12} + \frac{5}{x} = \frac{11}{6}\) by eliminating denominators and solving the resulting quadratic equation. Use a calculator as permitted.  
[/PLANNING]  
[IMP]  
Start with: \(\frac{20}{2x + 1} + \frac{5}{x} + \frac{1}{12} = \frac{11}{6}\).  
Move \(\frac{1}{12}\) to the right: \(\frac{20}{2x + 1} + \frac{5}{x} = \frac{11}{6} - \frac{1}{12}\).  
Common denominator for the right: \(\frac{11}{6} = \frac{22}{12}\), so \(\frac{22}{12} - \frac{1}{12} = \frac{21}{12} = \frac{7}{4}\).  
Equation becomes: \(\frac{20}{2x + 1} + \frac{5}{x} = \frac{7}{4}\).  
Multiply through by \(x(2x + 1) \cdot 4\) to clear denominators:  
- Left: \(4x(2x + 1) \cdot \frac{20}{2x + 1} + 4x(2x + 1) \cdot \frac{5}{x} = 80x + 20(2x + 1) \cdot 4 = 80x + 80x + 40 = 160x + 40\).  
- Right: \(4x(2x + 1) \cdot \frac{7}{4} = 7x(2x + 1) = 14x^2 + 7x\).  
Result: \(160x + 40 = 14x^2 + 7x\).  
Rearrange: \(14x^2 + 7x - 160x - 40 = 0\).  
Simplify: \(14x^2 - 153x - 40 = 0\).  
Divide by 7: \(2x^2 - \frac{153}{7}x - \frac{40}{7} = 0\).  
Multiply by 7 to clear fractions: \(14x^2 - 153x - 40 = 0\).  
Use the quadratic formula \(x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}\), where \(a = 14\), \(b = -153\), \(c = -40\):  
Discriminant: \(b^2 - 4ac = (-153)^2 - 4 \cdot 14 \cdot (-40) = 23409 + 2240 = 25649\).  
\(\sqrt{25649} \approx 160.153\) (using a calculator).  
\(x = \frac{153 \pm \sqrt{25649}}{28}\).  
Positive root: \(x = \frac{153 + 160.153}{28} \approx \frac{313.153}{28} \approx 11.183\).  
Negative root: \(x = \frac{153 - 160.153}{28} \approx \frac{-7.153}{28} < 0\) (discard as speed is positive).  
So, \(x \approx 11.183\).  
[/IMP]
