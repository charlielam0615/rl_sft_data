**Problem Statement**: Find \( y \) such that \(\dfrac{y^2 - 9y + 8}{y - 1} + \dfrac{3y^2 + 16y - 12}{3y - 2} = -3\).

### Step 2
[PLANNING]  
**Action**: Reach Goal (Implementation)  
**Goal**: Simplify the first fraction \(\dfrac{y^2 - 9y + 8}{y - 1}\).  
**How to achieve**: Factor the numerator \( y^2 - 9y + 8 \) and check for cancellation with the denominator \( y - 1 \).  
[/PLANNING]

[IMP]  
The numerator is \( y^2 - 9y + 8 \). We need two numbers that multiply to 8 and add to \(-9\). These are \(-1\) and \(-8\).  
Thus, \( y^2 - 9y + 8 = (y - 1)(y - 8) \).  
The fraction becomes:  
\[
\dfrac{(y - 1)(y - 8)}{y - 1}
\]  
Since \( y \neq 1 \) (to avoid division by zero), the \( y - 1 \) terms cancel, simplifying to:  
\[
y - 8
\]  
[/IMP]

### Step 3
[PLANNING]  
**Action**: Reach Goal (Implementation)  
**Goal**: Simplify the second fraction \(\dfrac{3y^2 + 16y - 12}{3y - 2}\).  
**How to achieve**: Factor the numerator \( 3y^2 + 16y - 12 \) and check for cancellation with the denominator \( 3y - 2 \).  
[/PLANNING]

[IMP]  
Factor \( 3y^2 + 16y - 12 \). Use the quadratic formula for \( ay^2 + by + c \), where \( a = 3 \), \( b = 16 \), \( c = -12 \):  
\[
y = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} = \frac{-16 \pm \sqrt{16^2 - 4 \cdot 3 \cdot (-12)}}{2 \cdot 3} = \frac{-16 \pm \sqrt{256 + 144}}{6} = \frac{-16 \pm \sqrt{400}}{6} = \frac{-16 \pm 20}{6}
\]  
\[
y = \frac{4}{6} = \frac{2}{3}, \quad y = \frac{-36}{6} = -6
\]  
Thus, \( 3y^2 + 16y - 12 = 3(y - \frac{2}{3})(y + 6) = (3y - 2)(y + 6) \).  
The fraction becomes:  
\[
\dfrac{(3y - 2)(y + 6)}{3y - 2}
\]  
Since \( y \neq \frac{2}{3} \) (to avoid division by zero), the \( 3y - 2 \) terms cancel, simplifying to:  
\[
y + 6
\]  
[/IMP]

### Step 4

**Action**: Reach Goal (Implementation)  
**Goal**: Combine the simplified fractions and set up the equation.  
**How to achieve**: Use the simplified forms \( y - 8 \) and \( y + 6 \), combine them, and set equal to \(-3\).  
[/PLANNING]

[IMP]  
The equation becomes:  
\[
(y - 8) + (y + 6) = -3
\]  
Combine like terms:  
\[
y - 8 + y + 6 = 2y - 2
\]  
Set equal to \(-3\):  
\[
2y - 2 = -3
\]  
[/IMP]

### Step 5

[IMP]  
\[
2y - 2 = -3

Add 2 to both sides:  
\[

\]  
Divide by 2:  
\[
y = -\frac{1}{2}
\]  
[/IMP]

### Step 6
[PLANNING]  
**Action**: Validation (Implementation)  
**Goal**: Verify the solution \( y = -\frac{1}{2} \) in the original equation.  
**How to achieve**: Substitute \( y = -\frac{1}{2} \) into the original fractions, compute each term, and check if their sum equals \(-3\). Ensure denominators are non-zero.  
[/PLANNING]

[IMP]  
Check denominators:  
- \( y - 1 = -\frac{1}{2} - 1 = -\frac{3}{2} \neq 0 \)  
- \( 3y - 2 = 3 \cdot (-\frac{1}{2}) - 2 = -\frac{3}{2} - 2 = -\frac{7}{2} \neq 0 \)  

First fraction:  
\[
y^2 - 9y + 8 = \left(-\frac{1}{2}\right)^2 - 9 \cdot \left(-\frac{1}{2}\right) + 8 = \frac{1}{4} + \frac{9}{2} + 8 = \frac{1}{4} + \frac{18}{4} + \frac{32}{4} = \frac{51}{4}
\]  
\[
\dfrac{\frac{51}{4}}{-\frac{3}{2}} = \frac{51}{4} \cdot \left(-\frac{2}{3}\right) = -\frac{102}{12} = -\frac{17}{2}
\]  

Second fraction:  
\[
3y^2 + 16y - 12 = 3 \cdot \left(-\frac{1}{2}\right)^2 + 16 \cdot \left(-\frac{1}{2}\right) - 12 = 3 \cdot \frac{1}{4} - 8 - 12 = \frac{3}{4} - 20 = \frac{3}{4} - \frac{80}{4} = -\frac{77}{4}
\]  
\[
\dfrac{-\frac{77}{4}}{-\frac{7}{2}} = -\frac{77}{4} \cdot \left(-\frac{2}{7}\right) = \frac{154}{28} = \frac{77}{14}
\]  

Sum:  
\[
-\frac{17}{2} + \frac{77}{14} = -\frac{119}{14} + \frac{77}{14} = -\frac{42}{14} = -3
\]  
The sum equals \(-3\), so the solution is valid.  
[/IMP]
