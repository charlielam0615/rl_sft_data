### Problem Statement
We need to find the area enclosed by the region defined by the equation \( x^2 + y^2 + 12x + 16y = 0 \). This equation resembles the general form of a circle, so we will determine if it represents a circle, identify its properties (center and radius), and calculate the enclosed area.

### Step 1: Forward Subgoaling
[PLANNING]
**Action: Forward Subgoaling (Planning)**  
Break down the problem into intermediate subgoals:  
1. Rewrite the given equation in the standard form of a circle by completing the square.  
2. Identify the center and radius of the circle from the standard form.  
3. Calculate the area using the formula for the area of a circle, \( A = \pi r^2 \).  
[/PLANNING]

### Step 2: Reach Goal
[PLANNING]
**Action: Reach Goal (Implementation)**  
**Current Goal**: Rewrite the equation \( x^2 + y^2 + 12x + 16y = 0 \) in the standard form of a circle.  
**How to Achieve**: Group the \( x \)- and \( y \)-terms, complete the square for each, and simplify to obtain the form \( (x - h)^2 + (y - k)^2 = r^2 \).  
[/PLANNING]

[IMP]
Start with the equation:  
\[ x^2 + y^2 + 12x + 16y = 0 \]

Group the \( x \)- and \( y \)-terms:  
\[ (x^2 + 12x) + (y^2 + 16y) = 0 \]

Complete the square for \( x \)-terms:  
\[ x^2 + 12x = (x^2 + 12x + 36) - 36 = (x + 6)^2 - 36 \]  
(Here, \( \frac{12}{2} = 6 \), and \( 6^2 = 36 \)).

Complete the square for \( y \)-terms:  
\[ y^2 + 16y = (y^2 + 16y + 64) - 64 = (y + 8)^2 - 64 \]  
(Here, \( \frac{16}{2} = 8 \), and \( 8^2 = 64 \)).

Substitute back into the equation:  
\[ (x + 6)^2 - 36 + (y + 8)^2 - 64 = 0 \]

Simplify:  
\[ (x + 6)^2 + (y + 8)^2 - 100 = 0 \]  
\[ (x + 6)^2 + (y + 8)^2 = 100 \]

This is the standard form of a circle, \( (x - h)^2 + (y - k)^2 = r^2 \), where the center is \( (h, k) = (-6, -8) \) and the radius is \( r = \sqrt{100} = 10 \).  
[/IMP]

### Step 3: Validation
[PLANNING]
**Action: Validation (Implementation)**  
**Current Goal**: Verify that the standard form \( (x + 6)^2 + (y + 8)^2 = 100 \) correctly represents the original equation.  
**How to Achieve**: Expand the standard form back to the general form and check if it matches \( x^2 + y^2 + 12x + 16y = 0 \).  
[/PLANNING]

[IMP]
Expand \( (x + 6)^2 + (y + 8)^2 = 100 \):  
\[ (x + 6)^2 = x^2 + 12x + 36 \]  
\[ (y + 8)^2 = y^2 + 16y + 64 \]

So:  
\[ x^2 + 12x + 36 + y^2 + 16y + 64 = 100 \]

Combine and simplify:  
\[ x^2 + y^2 + 12x + 16y + 100 = 100 \]  
\[ x^2 + y^2 + 12x + 16y = 0 \]

This matches the original equation, confirming the standard form is correct.  
[/IMP]

### Step 5: Reach Goal
[PLANNING]
**Action: Reach Goal (Implementation)**  
**Current Goal**: Calculate the area of the circle with radius 10.  
**How to Achieve**: Use the formula for the area of a circle, \( A = \pi r^2 \), with \( r = 10 \).  
[/PLANNING]

[IMP]
The radius is \( r = 10 \).  
Area \( A = \pi r^2 = \pi \cdot 10^2 = 100\pi \).  

Thus, the area enclosed by the circle is \( 100\pi \) square units.  
[/IMP]

### Final Answer
The area enclosed by the region defined by the equation \( x^2 + y^2 + 12x + 16y = 0 \) is \( 100\pi \) square units.
