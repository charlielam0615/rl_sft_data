### Problem Restatement
We need to find the area of a circle given that points \( A(3,5) \) and \( B(7,10) \) are the endpoints of its diameter. The area of a circle is given by \( A = \pi r^2 \), where \( r \) is the radius. Since the diameter is the segment \( AB \), the radius is half the length of \( AB \). We will calculate the length of \( AB \), determine the radius, and compute the area in terms of \( \pi \).

\[ \text{Distance} = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2} \]

### Step 3
[PLANNING]

[IMP]
The diameter is \( \sqrt{41} \). The radius \( r \) is:  
\[ r = \frac{\text{diameter}}{2} = \frac{\sqrt{41}}{2} \]  
[/IMP]

### Step 4
[PLANNING]
**Action: Reach Goal (Implementation)**  
**Meta-data**: The current goal is to compute the area of the circle using the formula \( A = \pi r^2 \), where the radius is \( \frac{\sqrt{41}}{2} \).  
[/PLANNING]

[IMP]
The radius is \( r = \frac{\sqrt{41}}{2} \). The area is:  
\[ A = \pi r^2 = \pi \left( \frac{\sqrt{41}}{2} \right)^2 = \pi \cdot \frac{41}{4} = \frac{41\pi}{4} \]  
The area of the circle is \( \frac{41\pi}{4} \) square units.
