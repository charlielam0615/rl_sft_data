### Problem Restatement
We need to evaluate the expression \(\frac{x^1 \cdot x^2 \cdot x^3 \cdots x^{15}}{x^2 \cdot x^4 \cdot x^6 \cdots x^{20}}\) when \(x = 2\). This is a product of powers of \(x\) in the numerator (exponents 1 to 15) divided by a product of powers of \(x\) in the denominator (exponents 2, 4, 6, ..., 20). We need to simplify the expression and compute its value at \(x = 2\).

### Step 2: Reach Goal
[PLANNING]

**Current Goal**: Simplify the numerator \(x^1 \cdot x^2 \cdot x^3 \cdots x^{15}\).  
**How to Achieve**: Use the product rule for exponents, which states that \(x^a \cdot x^b = x^{a+b}\). The numerator is a product of \(x\) raised to consecutive integer exponents from 1 to 15. Sum the exponents and express the product as a single power of \(x\).
[/PLANNING]

[IMP]
The numerator is \(x^1 \cdot x^2 \cdot x^3 \cdots x^{15}\). Using the product rule for exponents, we combine the exponents:

\[
x^1 \cdot x^2 \cdot x^3 \cdots x^{15} = x^{1 + 2 + 3 + \cdots + 15}
\]

The sum of the exponents is the sum of the first 15 positive integers. Using the formula for the sum of the first \(n\) positive integers, \(\frac{n(n+1)}{2}\), for \(n = 15\):

\[
1 + 2 + 3 + \cdots + 15 = \frac{15 \cdot 16}{2} = \frac{240}{2} = 120
\]

Thus, the numerator simplifies to:

\[
x^{1 + 2 + \cdots + 15} = x^{120}
\]
[/IMP]

### Step 3: Reach Goal

[IMP]
The denominator is \(x^2 \cdot x^4 \cdot x^6 \cdots x^{20}\). The exponents are the even numbers from 2 to 20: 2, 4, 6, ..., 20. This is an arithmetic sequence with:

- First term: 2
- Common difference: 2
- Last term: 20

To find the number of terms, note that the sequence can be written as \(2 \cdot 1, 2 \cdot 2, 2 \cdot 3, \ldots, 2 \cdot 10\), so there are 10 terms (since \(2 \cdot 10 = 20\)).

Rewrite the product:

\[
x^2 \cdot x^4 \cdot x^6 \cdots x^{20} = x^{2 + 4 + 6 + \cdots + 20}
\]

The exponents are 2, 4, 6, ..., 20. Sum these exponents:

\[
2 + 4 + 6 + \cdots + 20 = 2 \cdot (1 + 2 + 3 + \cdots + 10)
\]

The sum \(1 + 2 + \cdots + 10\) is:

\[
\frac{10 \cdot 11}{2} = \frac{110}{2} = 55
\]

Thus:

\[
2 + 4 + \cdots + 20 = 2 \cdot 55 = 110
\]

So, the denominator simplifies to:

\[
x^{2 + 4 + \cdots + 20} = x^{110}
\]
[/IMP]
