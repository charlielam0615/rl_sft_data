[IMP]  
Start with the given equation:  
\[ \binom{20}{n} + \binom{20}{10} = \binom{21}{11} \]

Use the binomial identity \( \binom{m+1}{k} = \binom{m}{k} + \binom{m}{k-1} \):  
\[ \binom{21}{11} = \binom{20}{11} + \binom{20}{10} \]

Substitute into the equation:  
\[ \binom{20}{n} + \binom{20}{10} = \binom{20}{11} + \binom{20}{10} \]

Subtract \( \binom{20}{10} \) from both sides:  
\[ \binom{20}{n} = \binom{20}{11} \]

Since \( \binom{20}{k} = \binom{20}{20-k} \), if \( \binom{20}{n} = \binom{20}{11} \), then either:  
- \( n = 11 \), or  
- \( n = 20 - 11 = 9 \).  

Thus, possible values for \( n \) are \( n = 9 \) or \( n = 11 \).  
[/IMP]
