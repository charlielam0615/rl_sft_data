### Step 3: Reach Goal
[PLANNING]  
**Action**: Reach Goal (Implementation)  
**Meta-data**:  
- **Current Goal**: Clean the boat.  
- **How to Achieve**: Remove dirt, grime, and old wax from the boat’s surface using a marine cleaner and a scrub brush to ensure a clean base for subsequent steps.  
[/PLANNING]  

[IMP]  
To clean the boat, use a marine-grade cleaner suitable for the boat’s material (e.g., fiberglass). Dilute the cleaner as per instructions, apply it with a soft scrub brush, and rinse thoroughly with fresh water. Ensure all surfaces are free of dirt, grease, and old wax, focusing on areas where the gel coat will be applied. Dry the boat with a clean cloth to prevent water spots.  
[/IMP]

**Meta-data**:

- **How to Achieve**: Inspect the boat’s surface for any remaining dirt, grease, or wax to confirm it is ready for the next step (treating holes or cracks).

[IMP]  
Visually inspect the boat’s surface under good lighting to ensure no residue remains. Run a gloved hand over the surface to check for smoothness; any slick or rough areas indicate incomplete cleaning. If residue is found, reapply the marine cleaner, scrub, and rinse again. Confirm the surface is dry and clean before proceeding.

### Step 5: Reach Goal
[PLANNING]  
**Action**: Reach Goal (Implementation)  
**Meta-data**:  
- **Current Goal**: Treat holes or cracks on the boat.  
- **How to Achieve**: Identify and prepare holes or cracks using sandpaper to smooth edges and remove loose material, ensuring a stable base for filling.  
[/PLANNING]  

[IMP]  
Inspect the boat for visible holes or cracks. Use medium-grit sandpaper (e.g., 80-grit) to sand around each defect, removing loose or damaged material. Clean the sanded areas with a solvent like acetone to remove dust and ensure a clean surface for filling. Wear gloves and work in a well-ventilated area when using solvents.  
[/IMP]

---

### Step 6: Reach Goal
[PLANNING]  
**Action**: Reach Goal (Implementation)  
**Meta-data**:  
- **Current Goal**: Fill holes on the boat.  
- **How to Achieve**: Apply marine-grade filler to the treated holes and cracks, smoothing the surface to prepare for polishing.  
[/PLANNING]  

[IMP]  
Mix a marine-grade epoxy or polyester filler according to the manufacturer’s instructions. Apply the filler to each hole or crack using a putty knife, ensuring it is slightly overfilled to account for shrinkage. Smooth the surface with the knife and allow the filler to cure as specified (typically 24 hours). Once cured, sand the filled areas with fine-grit sandpaper (e.g., 120-grit) to level them with the surrounding surface.  
[/IMP]

---

### Step 7: Validation
[PLANNING]  
**Action**: Validation (Implementation)  
**Meta-data**:  
- **Current Goal**: Ensure holes are properly filled.  
- **How to Achieve**: Check the filled areas for smoothness and adhesion, ensuring they blend seamlessly with the boat’s surface.  
[/PLANNING]  

[IMP]  
Inspect the filled areas visually and by touch to ensure they are level with the surrounding surface. Sand any high spots with fine-grit sandpaper (e.g., 120-grit) and clean with acetone to remove dust. If any gaps or uneven areas remain, apply additional filler, let it cure, and sand again. Confirm the surface is smooth and ready for polishing.  
[/IMP]

---

### Step 8: Reach Goal
[PLANNING]  
**Action**: Reach Goal (Implementation)  
**Meta-data**:  
- **Current Goal**: Polish the surface of the boat.  
- **How to Achieve**: Use a marine polishing compound and a buffer to create a smooth, even surface for gel coat application.  
[/PLANNING]  

[IMP]  
Apply a marine polishing compound to the boat’s surface using a clean cloth or a low-speed buffer. Work in small sections, using circular motions to remove minor scratches and create a uniform surface. Wipe off excess compound with a clean cloth and inspect for a glossy, smooth finish. Clean the surface with a solvent like acetone to remove any remaining residue before gel coat application.  
[/IMP]

---

### Step 9: Reach Goal
[PLANNING]  
**Action**: Reach Goal (Implementation)  
**Meta-data**:  
- **Current Goal**: Mix the gel coat.  
- **How to Achieve**: Combine the gel coat with a hardener in the correct ratio to prepare it for brush application.  
[/PLANNING]  

[IMP]  
In a clean container, mix the gel coat with the appropriate hardener (typically 1-2% by volume, as per manufacturer instructions). Stir thoroughly for 1-2 minutes to ensure even consistency, avoiding air bubbles. Prepare only the amount needed for immediate use, as gel coat has a limited working time (usually 15-30 minutes). Work in a well-ventilated area and wear protective gloves and a mask.  
[/IMP]

---

### Step 10: Reach Goal
[PLANNING]  
**Action**: Reach Goal (Implementation)  
**Meta-data**:  
- **Current Goal**: Apply the gel coat with a brush.  
- **How to Achieve**: Use a clean brush to apply an even layer of mixed gel coat to the boat’s surface, following the polished areas.  
[/PLANNING]  

[IMP]  
Using a high-quality, clean brush, apply the mixed gel coat in thin, even layers to the polished surface. Work in small sections to maintain control, using long, smooth strokes to avoid streaks or bubbles. Apply multiple coats (typically 2-3) if needed, allowing each coat to become tacky (per manufacturer instructions) before applying the next. Ensure the workspace is dust-free to prevent contamination.  
[/IMP]

### Step 11: Validation
[PLANNING]  
**Action**: Validation (Implementation)  
**Meta-data**:  
- **Current Goal**: Ensure the gel coat is applied correctly.  
- **How to Achieve**: Inspect the gel-coated surface for evenness, coverage, and absence of defects like bubbles or streaks.  
[/PLANNING]  

[IMP]  
Examine the gel-coated surface under good lighting for uniform thickness and coverage. Check for bubbles, streaks, or missed spots. If defects are found, lightly sand the affected area with fine-grit sandpaper (e.g., 400-grit), clean with acetone, and apply an additional thin layer of gel coat. Allow the gel coat to cure fully (typically 24 hours) before proceeding.  
[/IMP]

[IMP]  
Apply a fine-grade marine polishing compound to the cured gel coat using a clean cloth or a low-speed buffer. Work in small sections with circular motions to achieve a glossy finish. Remove excess compound with a clean cloth and inspect for a smooth, reflective surface. Clean the surface with a mild cleaner to remove any polishing residue.  
[/IMP]

### Step 13: Reach Goal

Allow the wax to haze (as per manufacturer instructions), then buff with a clean microfiber cloth to achieve a protective, glossy finish. Inspect for complete coverage and reapply if necessary.  
[/IMP]
