Hold the trowel at a 45-degree angle to create consistent ridges in the mortar, which will help with tile adhesion. Work in small sections to prevent the mortar from drying out before tiles are placed. Keep a damp cloth nearby to clean any excess mortar from tools or the wall.

Continue placing tiles row by row, working outward and upward, following the reference lines and layout plan. Check periodically with a level to ensure the tiles are straight and even. Cut tiles can be placed in their designated spots as you go. If excess mortar squeezes out between tiles, clean it immediately with a damp cloth. Allow the mortar to set for 24 hours before proceeding.

**Step 24:**  
[PLANNING]  
Action: Reach Goal (Implementation Category)  
Current Goal: Grout the entire wall  
Description: The goal is to apply grout between the tiles to fill gaps and provide a finished look. This will be achieved by mixing grout and using a float to apply it.  
[/PLANNING]  
[IMP]  
Wait at least 24 hours after laying the tiles to ensure the mortar has set. Mix the grout according to the manufacturer’s instructions, achieving a smooth, peanut butter-like consistency. Using a rubber grout float, scoop a small amount of grout and apply it over the tiles at a 45-degree angle, pushing it into the gaps between tiles. Work in small sections (about 3-4 square feet) to ensure even application. After 15-30 minutes, use a damp sponge to wipe away excess grout from the tile surfaces, rinsing the sponge frequently. Continue until all gaps are filled and the tiles are clean. Allow the grout to cure for the recommended time (usually 24-48 hours) before sealing.  
[/IMP]

**Step 27:**  
[PLANNING]  
Action: Reach Goal (Implementation Category)  
Current Goal: Seal the tiles neatly  
Description: The goal is to apply a sealant to the tiles and grout to protect them from moisture and stains. This will be achieved by using a brush or sponge to apply the sealant evenly.  
[/PLANNING]  
[IMP]  
Ensure the grout has cured for the recommended time (typically 24-48 hours) before sealing. Choose a tile and grout sealant appropriate for your materials (check manufacturer recommendations). Using a small brush, sponge, or applicator, apply the sealant evenly over the tiles and grout lines, working in small sections to avoid over-application. Follow the product instructions for application and drying times—most sealants require 4-6 hours to dry before a second coat, if needed. Wipe away any excess sealant from the tile surfaces with a clean cloth before it dries. Allow the sealant to cure fully (often 24-72 hours) before exposing the backsplash to water or heavy use.  
[/IMP]

---

**Step 28:**  
[PLANNING]  
Action: Validation (Implementation Category)  
Current Goal: Validate the sealing of the tiles  
Description: The goal is to ensure the sealant is applied properly and the backsplash is protected. This will be done by inspecting the coverage and testing for water resistance.  
[/PLANNING]  
[IMP]  
Inspect the entire backsplash to confirm that the sealant covers all tiles and grout lines evenly without missed spots or pooling. Check for any shiny or uneven areas that might indicate improper application; if found, apply additional sealant to those areas as needed. After the sealant has cured (per manufacturer instructions, typically 24-72 hours), test water resistance by splashing a small amount of water on the backsplash—water should bead up and not penetrate the grout or tiles. Wipe the area dry and ensure no residue or damage is visible. If water absorbs into the grout, apply another coat of sealant.  
[/IMP]
