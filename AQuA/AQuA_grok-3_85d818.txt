**Problem Restatement:**
A, B, and C each invest Rs. 20,000 to start a business. After 5 months, A withdraws Rs. 6,000, B withdraws Rs. 4,000, and C invests an additional Rs. 6,000. At the end of the year (12 months), the total profit is Rs. 69,900. We need to determine the profit share of A, B, and C based on their investments and the time they were invested.

---

### Step 1
[PLANNING]
**Action: Forward Subgoaling (Planning)**  
Break down the problem into intermediate subgoals to solve for the profit shares.

To find each partner's share of the profit, we need to:
1. Calculate the capital contribution of each partner over time, accounting for withdrawals and additional investments.
2. Determine the time-weighted capital (capital × months) for each partner.
3. Compute the ratio of their contributions to the total contribution.
4. Allocate the total profit of Rs. 69,900 according to this ratio.
[/PLANNING]

### Step 2
[PLANNING]
**Action: Reach Goal (Implementation)**  
**Meta-data:** The current goal is to calculate the time-weighted capital contributions of A, B, and C. This involves determining each partner’s investment amounts before and after the 5-month mark, multiplying by the respective time periods, and summing the contributions.
[/PLANNING]

[IMP]
- **A’s contribution:**
  - For the first 5 months: Rs. 20,000 × 5 = 100,000
  - After withdrawing Rs. 6,000, investment becomes Rs. 14,000
  - For the remaining 7 months: Rs. 14,000 × 7 = 98,000
  - Total for A: 100,000 + 98,000 = 198,000

- **B’s contribution:**
  - For the first 5 months: Rs. 20,000 × 5 = 100,000
  - After withdrawing Rs. 4,000, investment becomes Rs. 16,000
  - For the remaining 7 months: Rs. 16,000 × 7 = 112,000
  - Total for B: 100,000 + 112,000 = 212,000

- **C’s contribution:**
  - For the first 5 months: Rs. 20,000 × 5 = 100,000
  - After adding Rs. 6,000, investment becomes Rs. 26,000
  - For the remaining 7 months: Rs. 26,000 × 7 = 182,000
  - Total for C: 100,000 + 182,000 = 282,000
[/IMP]

### Step 4

[IMP]

- Ratio of contributions (A : B : C) = 198,000 : 212,000 : 282,000
- Simplify by dividing by 2,000: 99 : 106 : 141
- Total parts = 99 + 106 + 141 = 346
- Profit per part = 69,900 ÷ 346 ≈ 201.7341
- A’s share: 99 × 201.7341 ≈ 19,971.68
- B’s share: 106 × 201.7341 ≈ 21,383.81
- C’s share: 141 × 201.7341 ≈ 28,444.51
